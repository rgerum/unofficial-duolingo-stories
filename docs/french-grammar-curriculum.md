# French Grammar Curriculum — CEFR-Aligned Topic Inventory

This document catalogues French grammar topics by CEFR level and sublevel (Pre-A1 through C2). The goal is a comprehensive inventory that ensures every major grammar point has a home, so nothing falls through the cracks when we design weekly curriculum cycles.

Sublevels follow the CEFR Companion Volume (2020), which defines "plus levels" (A2+, B1+, B2+) as the strong end of each band — learners who have consolidated the level but are not yet at the next. Pre-A1 was added in the same volume for true beginners.

Sources: CEFR Companion Volume (2020), Council of Europe Reference Level Descriptions for French, Threshold/Waystage/Vantage specifications, and standard French language pedagogy.

---

## Topic Metadata

Each topic includes:

- **Category** — grammatical category, used for the alternation rule (no more than 2 consecutive same-category weeks in a cycle).
- **Prerequisites** — topics that must be introduced before this one, either earlier in the same cycle or in a prior sublevel. Listed as `Topic name (level)`.
- **Cluster** — pedagogical cluster name, if any. Clustered topics should appear within 1–2 weeks of each other.
- **News relevance** — marked `high` for topics that naturally appear in news text. Only listed when notably high; omitted means standard/low.

### Category Definitions

| Category | Covers |
|----------|--------|
| `foundation` | Pre-A1 vocabulary-as-grammar chunks |
| `verb-form` | Tense/mood conjugation and formation |
| `verb-construction` | Periphrastic and complex verb structures (aller+inf, faire causatif, gérondif, etc.) |
| `pronoun` | All pronoun types including Y/en, double pronouns |
| `determiner` | Articles, demonstrative/possessive/interrogative/indefinite adjectives |
| `modifier` | Adjective/adverb properties, comparative/superlative, quantity expressions |
| `sentence` | Sentence-level patterns: questions, negation, imperative, exclamative, passive |
| `clause` | Multi-clause constructions: relative clauses, reported speech, conditionals, embedded questions |
| `connector` | Linking words and phrases (cause, consequence, concession, temporal, discourse markers) |
| `expression` | Fixed/semi-fixed expressions (avoir+noun), impersonal constructions |
| `discourse` | Text-level and register features: nominalisation, register shifting, emphasis, mise en relief |

## Pre-A1 — Foundation / Pré-découverte

Learner can recognise isolated words, very basic fixed phrases, and familiar visual cues. Not yet able to form sentences.

1. **Alphabet and spelling** — French alphabet, accents (é, è, ê, ë, ç, à, ù, î, ô, û), basic spelling-to-sound rules
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

2. **Greetings and politeness formulas** — bonjour, merci, s'il vous plaît, au revoir, excusez-moi (as fixed chunks, not analysed grammar)
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

3. **Single-word responses** — oui, non, d'accord, voilà, peut-être
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

4. **Subject pronouns — recognition** — je, tu, il/elle, nous, vous, ils/elles (identifying who is speaking)
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

5. **Être and avoir — je suis, j'ai only** — first-person fixed forms as vocabulary items
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

6. **Basic nouns with articles as fixed units** — le chat, une maison, l'eau (article + noun learned as one chunk)
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

7. **Cardinal numbers 0–20** — counting, recognising written numerals
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

8. **Days of the week, months** — as vocabulary, not grammatical structures
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

9. **Basic question words** — qui?, quoi?, où?, comment? (as isolated words, not in sentence frames)
   - Category: `foundation`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: —

## A1 — Breakthrough / Découverte

Learner can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases.

1. **Articles: definite, indefinite, partitive** — le/la/les, un/une/des, du/de la/des; article choice and contractions (au, du); partitive in negative (pas de/d')
   - Category: `determiner`
   - Prerequisites: Basic nouns with articles (Pre-A1)
   - Cluster: `articles-determiners`

2. **Present tense: regular verbs** — -er, -ir, -re conjugation patterns
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Subject pronouns (Pre-A1)
   - Cluster: `present-tense`

3. **Être and avoir — full present tense** — all persons, usage as main verbs and auxiliaries
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Être and avoir je suis/j'ai (Pre-A1)
   - Cluster: `present-tense`

4. **Common irregular verbs in present** — faire, aller, venir, prendre, pouvoir, vouloir, devoir, savoir
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
   - Cluster: `present-tense`

5. **Negation** — ne...pas (basic placement with simple and compound verbs)
   - Category: `sentence`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
   - Cluster: —

6. **Gender and number agreement** — noun/adjective agreement basics, plural formation (-s, -x, -aux)
   - Category: `modifier`
   - Prerequisites: Articles (A1)
   - Cluster: `agreement-basics`

7. **Adjective placement and agreement** — pre-nominal (grand, petit, bon, beau, joli) vs post-nominal, regular agreement patterns
   - Category: `modifier`
   - Prerequisites: Gender and number agreement (A1)
   - Cluster: `agreement-basics`

8. **Question formation** — intonation, est-ce que, basic inversion, question tags (n'est-ce pas)
   - Category: `sentence`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
   - Cluster: `questions`

9. **Interrogative adjectives** — quel/quelle/quels/quelles (Quel âge as-tu? Quelle heure est-il?)
   - Category: `determiner`
   - Prerequisites: Question formation (A1)
   - Cluster: `questions`

10. **Possessive adjectives** — mon/ma/mes, ton/ta/tes, son/sa/ses, notre/nos, votre/vos, leur/leurs
    - Category: `determiner`
    - Prerequisites: Gender and number agreement (A1)
    - Cluster: —

11. **Stress pronouns (pronoms toniques)** — moi, toi, lui, elle, nous, vous, eux, elles; after prepositions (avec moi), for emphasis (moi, je...), after c'est (c'est lui)
    - Category: `pronoun`
    - Prerequisites: Subject pronouns (Pre-A1)
    - Cluster: —

12. **Prepositions of place** — à, en, dans, sur, sous, chez, devant, derrière, entre, près de, loin de
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: none
    - Cluster: `prepositions`

13. **Prepositions with countries, cities, regions** — en France, au Japon, aux États-Unis, à Paris, en Normandie
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: Articles (A1)
    - Cluster: `prepositions`

14. **Il y a / c'est vs il est** — existence, identification, and description
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: Être and avoir full present (A1)
    - Cluster: —

15. **Aller + infinitive (futur proche)** — near future
    - Category: `verb-construction`
    - Prerequisites: Common irregular verbs — aller (A1)
    - Cluster: `periphrastic-tenses`

16. **Venir de + infinitive (passé récent)** — recent past
    - Category: `verb-construction`
    - Prerequisites: Common irregular verbs — venir (A1)
    - Cluster: `periphrastic-tenses`

17. **Expressions of quantity** — beaucoup de, peu de, un peu de, trop de, assez de, plus de, moins de, combien de
    - Category: `modifier`
    - Prerequisites: Articles — partitive (A1)
    - Cluster: —

18. **Numbers, dates, time expressions** — cardinal numbers to 1000, ordinals, days, months, telling time
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: Cardinal numbers 0–20 (Pre-A1)
    - Cluster: —

19. **Expressions with avoir** — avoir faim/soif/chaud/froid/peur/sommeil/raison/tort, avoir besoin de, avoir envie de, avoir l'air + adj (idiomatic: French uses "have" where English uses "be")
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: Être and avoir full present (A1)
    - Cluster: —

20. **Impersonal verbs and constructions** — il fait beau/chaud/froid, il pleut/neige, il faut + infinitive (necessity), il est possible/important de + infinitive
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: Être and avoir full present (A1)
    - Cluster: —

21. **Imperative (basic)** — tu/nous/vous forms for regular and common irregular verbs
    - Category: `sentence`
    - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
    - Cluster: —

22. **Basic adverbs** — bien, mal, vite, très, beaucoup, peu, souvent, toujours, jamais
    - Category: `modifier`
    - Prerequisites: none
    - Cluster: —

## A2 — Waystage / Survie

Learner can communicate in simple, routine tasks and describe aspects of their background and immediate environment.

1. **Passé composé with avoir** — formation, common irregular past participles (fait, pris, dit, écrit, vu, bu, lu, mis)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Être and avoir full present (A1)
   - Cluster: `past-tenses`

2. **Passé composé with être** — movement verbs (DR MRS VANDERTRAMP), agreement of past participle
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Passé composé with avoir (A2), Être and avoir full present (A1), Gender and number agreement (A1)
   - Cluster: `past-tenses`

3. **Imparfait** — formation and usage (description, habits, background states)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
   - Cluster: `past-tenses`

4. **Reflexive verbs in present tense** — se lever, s'appeler, se sentir; placement of pronoun
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1), Stress pronouns (A1)
   - Cluster: —

5. **Direct object pronouns** — le, la, les (placement before verb, agreement in passé composé)
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Passé composé with avoir (A2)
   - Cluster: `object-pronouns`

6. **Indirect object pronouns** — lui, leur (placement and distinction from direct objects)
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Direct object pronouns (A2)
   - Cluster: `object-pronouns`

7. **Relative pronouns (basic)** — qui (subject), que (object)
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
   - Cluster: —

8. **Demonstrative adjectives** — ce/cet/cette/ces
   - Category: `determiner`
   - Prerequisites: Articles (A1), Gender and number agreement (A1)
   - Cluster: `articles-determiners`

9. **Indefinite adjectives and pronouns** — chaque, tout/toute/tous/toutes, plusieurs, quelques, quelqu'un, quelque chose, tout le monde
   - Category: `determiner`
   - Prerequisites: Articles (A1), Gender and number agreement (A1)
   - Cluster: `articles-determiners`

10. **Adverb formation in -ment** — lentement, facilement, vraiment, heureusement; formation rules from adjectives
    - Category: `modifier`
    - Prerequisites: Adjective placement and agreement (A1), Basic adverbs (A1)
    - Cluster: —

11. **Prepositions with infinitives (à/de)** — commencer à, finir de, essayer de, réussir à, hésiter à, apprendre à, oublier de, décider de
    - Category: `verb-construction`
    - Prerequisites: Common irregular verbs in present (A1)
    - Cluster: —

12. **Être en train de + infinitive** — present continuous (je suis en train de manger), contrast with simple present
    - Category: `verb-construction`
    - Prerequisites: Être and avoir full present (A1)
    - Cluster: —

13. **Basic connectors** — mais, ou, et, donc, parce que, car, alors, puis, ensuite, d'abord
    - Category: `connector`
    - Prerequisites: none
    - Cluster: —

14. **Depuis / pendant / pour** — expressing duration and time frames
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1), Passé composé with avoir (A2)
    - Cluster: —

## A2+ — Strong Waystage / Survie avancée

Learner can handle most routine situations with some confidence and begin to narrate in the past.

1. **Passé composé vs imparfait** — aspect distinction in past narration (event vs background)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Passé composé with avoir (A2), Passé composé with être (A2), Imparfait (A2)
   - Cluster: —

2. **Reflexive verbs in passé composé** — auxiliary être, agreement rules (elle s'est lavée vs elle s'est lavé les mains)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Reflexive verbs in present tense (A2), Passé composé with être (A2)
   - Cluster: —

3. **Comparative and superlative** — plus/moins/aussi...que, le plus/le moins, irregular forms (meilleur, mieux, pire)
   - Category: `modifier`
   - Prerequisites: Adjective placement and agreement (A1), Adverb formation in -ment (A2)
   - Cluster: `comparison`

4. **Futur simple** — formation, common irregular stems (ser-, aur-, fer-, ir-, pourr-, voudr-, saur-, devr-)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1), Common irregular verbs in present (A1)
   - Cluster: `expanded-tenses`

5. **Conditional present** — formation, polite requests (je voudrais), hypothetical basics
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Futur simple (A2+)
   - Cluster: `expanded-tenses`

6. **Y and en** — pronominal adverbs (basic usage: j'y vais, j'en veux, j'en ai trois)
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Direct object pronouns (A2), Indirect object pronouns (A2), Prepositions of place (A1)
   - Cluster: —

7. **Imperative with pronouns** — affirmative/negative placement (donne-le-moi, ne le fais pas)
   - Category: `sentence`
   - Prerequisites: Imperative basic (A1), Direct object pronouns (A2)
   - Cluster: —

8. **Demonstrative pronouns** — celui/celle/ceux/celles, celui-ci/celui-là
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Demonstrative adjectives (A2)
   - Cluster: —

9. **Possessive pronouns** — le mien, la tienne, le sien, les nôtres, les vôtres, les leurs
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Possessive adjectives (A1)
   - Cluster: —

10. **Negation (expanded)** — ne...plus, ne...jamais, ne...rien, ne...personne, ne...ni...ni, ne...que (restrictive)
    - Category: `sentence`
    - Prerequisites: Negation ne...pas (A1)
    - Cluster: `expanded-negation`

11. **Adverb placement in compound tenses** — short adverbs between auxiliary and participle (j'ai bien mangé, il a beaucoup parlé) vs long adverbs after (j'ai mangé lentement)
    - Category: `modifier`
    - Prerequisites: Passé composé with avoir (A2), Adverb formation in -ment (A2)
    - Cluster: —

12. **Exclamative sentences** — Quel beau temps! Comme c'est beau! Que c'est joli! Qu'est-ce qu'il fait froid!
    - Category: `sentence`
    - Prerequisites: Interrogative adjectives (A1), Adjective placement and agreement (A1)
    - Cluster: —

## B1 — Threshold / Seuil

Learner can deal with most situations likely to arise while travelling and can produce simple connected text on familiar topics.

1. **Plus-que-parfait** — formation and usage (past before past, narrative sequencing)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Passé composé vs imparfait (A2+), Imparfait (A2)
   - Cluster: —

2. **Conditional sentences (types 1 & 2)** — si + présent → futur; si + imparfait → conditionnel présent
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Futur simple (A2+), Conditional present (A2+), Imparfait (A2)
   - Cluster: —

3. **Subjonctif présent — formation** — regular and irregular stems (fasse, aille, soit, ait, puisse, sache)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1), Common irregular verbs in present (A1)
   - Cluster: `subjunctive-intro`

4. **Subjonctif — common triggers** — il faut que, je veux que, pour que, avant que, bien que, à moins que
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif présent formation (B1)
   - Cluster: `subjunctive-intro`

5. **Passive voice** — formation with être + past participle, agent with par/de
   - Category: `sentence`
   - Prerequisites: Passé composé with être (A2), Gender and number agreement (A1)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

6. **Reported speech (discours indirect) — basics** — il dit que, elle a demandé si, tense shift in present reporting
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1), Question formation (A1)
   - Cluster: `reported-speech`
   - News relevance: `high`

7. **Relative pronouns (intermediate)** — dont (possession/about), où (place/time)
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Relative pronouns basic (A2)
   - Cluster: —

8. **Cause expressions** — grâce à, à cause de, puisque, comme, car
   - Category: `connector`
   - Prerequisites: Basic connectors (A2)
   - Cluster: `cause-consequence`
   - News relevance: `high`

9. **Gérondif** — en + participe présent (simultaneity, manner, condition)
   - Category: `verb-construction`
   - Prerequisites: Present tense regular verbs (A1)
   - Cluster: —

10. **Expressing opinion** — je pense que, je crois que, il me semble que, à mon avis, d'après moi
    - Category: `expression`
    - Prerequisites: none
    - Cluster: —

11. **Indirect/embedded questions** — je ne sais pas où il est, dis-moi ce que tu veux, je me demande si elle viendra (word order differences from direct questions)
    - Category: `clause`
    - Prerequisites: Question formation (A1)
    - Cluster: —

## B1+ — Strong Threshold / Seuil avancé

Learner can maintain interaction and express ideas on abstract topics with reasonable fluency, beginning to handle more complex structures.

1. **Reported speech — full tense shifting** — tense backshift in past reporting (il a dit qu'il viendrait)
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Reported speech basics (B1), Imparfait (A2), Conditional present (A2+), Plus-que-parfait (B1)
   - Cluster: `reported-speech`
   - News relevance: `high`

2. **Futur antérieur** — formation and usage (quand j'aurai fini, je partirai)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Futur simple (A2+), Passé composé with avoir (A2)
   - Cluster: —

3. **Consequence expressions** — donc, c'est pourquoi, si bien que, de sorte que, par conséquent
   - Category: `connector`
   - Prerequisites: Cause expressions (B1), Basic connectors (A2)
   - Cluster: `cause-consequence`
   - News relevance: `high`

4. **Temporal connectors** — dès que, aussitôt que, avant de + infinitif, avant que + subj, après + infinitif passé
   - Category: `connector`
   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif common triggers (B1), Basic connectors (A2)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

5. **Y and en in complex contexts** — combined with other pronouns, idiomatic verbs (s'en aller, s'y mettre)
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Y and en basic (A2+), Direct object pronouns (A2), Indirect object pronouns (A2)
   - Cluster: —

6. **Nominalisation (basics)** — verb → noun (décider → la décision, arriver → l'arrivée, développer → le développement)
   - Category: `discourse`
   - Prerequisites: none
   - Cluster: `nominalisation`
   - News relevance: `high`

7. **Relative pronouns (advanced)** — ce qui, ce que, ce dont, ce à quoi
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Relative pronouns intermediate (B1), Relative pronouns basic (A2)
   - Cluster: —

8. **Expressing opinion formally** — selon moi, d'après les experts, il paraît que, il s'avère que
   - Category: `expression`
   - Prerequisites: Expressing opinion (B1)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

9. **Faire causatif** — faire + infinitive (je fais réparer la voiture, elle fait manger les enfants), pronoun placement
   - Category: `verb-construction`
   - Prerequisites: Direct object pronouns (A2), Indirect object pronouns (A2)
   - Cluster: `causative-perception`

10. **Laisser and perception verbs + infinitive** — je le laisse partir, je l'entends chanter, je la vois arriver
    - Category: `verb-construction`
    - Prerequisites: Direct object pronouns (A2)
    - Cluster: `causative-perception`

## B2 — Vantage / Avancé

Learner can interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity, produce clear detailed text, and explain a viewpoint giving advantages and disadvantages.

1. **Subjonctif passé** — formation and usage (je suis content qu'il soit venu)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif présent formation (B1), Passé composé with avoir (A2), Passé composé with être (A2)
   - Cluster: `advanced-past`

2. **Conditionnel passé** — regret, reproach, unrealised past (j'aurais dû, il aurait pu)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Conditional present (A2+), Passé composé with avoir (A2)
   - Cluster: `advanced-past`

3. **Conditional sentences (type 3)** — si + plus-que-parfait → conditionnel passé
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Conditionnel passé (B2), Plus-que-parfait (B1), Conditional sentences types 1 & 2 (B1)
   - Cluster: `advanced-past`

4. **Concession** — bien que + subj, malgré, quoique, avoir beau, même si
   - Category: `connector`
   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif common triggers (B1)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

5. **Passive voice — advanced** — pronominal passive (ça se dit, ça se fait), impersonal passive (il a été décidé)
   - Category: `sentence`
   - Prerequisites: Passive voice (B1), Reflexive verbs in present tense (A2)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

6. **Double object pronouns** — order and placement (il me le donne, ne le lui dis pas, je le lui ai donné)
   - Category: `pronoun`
   - Prerequisites: Direct object pronouns (A2), Indirect object pronouns (A2), Y and en basic (A2+)
   - Cluster: —

7. **Passé simple — recognition** — recognition for reading news and literature (not active production)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Passé composé vs imparfait (A2+)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

8. **Conditionnel journalistique** — le président serait malade, il y aurait eu un accident (reported/unconfirmed information in news)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Conditional present (A2+), Conditionnel passé (B2)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

## B2+ — Strong Vantage / Avancé supérieur

Learner can express themselves at length, take an active part in discussion, and account for and sustain viewpoint with relevant explanations and arguments.

   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif common triggers (B1)
   - Cluster: —

3. **Nuanced connectors** — en revanche, d'ailleurs, quant à, or, certes, néanmoins, toutefois, cependant
   - Category: `connector`
   - Prerequisites: Basic connectors (A2), Cause expressions (B1), Consequence expressions (B1+)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

4. **Advanced nominalisation** — verb/adjective → noun transformations in formal writing (augmenter → l'augmentation, efficace → l'efficacité)
   - Category: `discourse`
   - Prerequisites: Nominalisation basics (B1+)
   - Cluster: `nominalisation`
   - News relevance: `high`

5. **Participial clauses** — étant donné (que), une fois terminé, ayant fini, vu les circonstances
   - Category: `clause`
   - Prerequisites: Gérondif (B1), Passé composé with avoir (A2)

   - News relevance: `high`

6. **Hypothesis and speculation** — au cas où + conditionnel, à supposer que + subj, dans l'hypothèse où
   - Category: `clause`

7. **Discourse structuring** — d'une part...d'autre part, en somme, en définitive, bref, tout compte fait
   - Category: `discourse`
   - Prerequisites: Nuanced connectors (B2+)
   - Cluster: —
   - News relevance: `high`

## C1 — Effective Operational Proficiency / Autonome

Learner can express ideas fluently and spontaneously, use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes.

1. **Subjonctif imparfait — recognition** — literary/formal forms (qu'il fût, qu'il eût, qu'il allât)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif présent formation (B1), Imparfait (A2)
   - Cluster: `literary-tenses`

2. **Subjonctif plus-que-parfait — recognition** — literary (qu'il eût été, qu'il fût venu)
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Subjonctif imparfait recognition (C1), Plus-que-parfait (B1)
   - Cluster: `literary-tenses`

3. **Passé simple — active control** — production in written narrative and formal contexts
   - Category: `verb-form`
   - Prerequisites: Passé simple recognition (B2)
   - Cluster: `literary-tenses`

   - Category: `verb-form`

## Notes

- **Sublevels follow the CEFR Companion Volume (2020).** Pre-A1, A2+, B1+, and B2+ are officially recognised in the CEFR framework. C1 and C2 do not have official "plus" sublevels.
- **Overlap between levels is intentional.** A topic introduced at one level (e.g., subjunctive at B1) is deepened at the next (B2 subjunctive passé, C1 literary subjunctive forms). The weekly curriculum should mark whether a topic is "introduce," "reinforce," or "deepen."
- **This is a topic inventory, not a sequence.** The order within each level does not imply teaching order. Sequencing comes next, informed by pedagogical dependencies (e.g., passé composé before plus-que-parfait).
- **News context affects emphasis.** Some topics (passive voice, reported speech, formal connectors, conditionnel journalistique) appear more in news text than in everyday conversation, so they may get natural reinforcement from the story content.
- **The "plus" sublevels serve as consolidation phases.** They take the harder or more nuanced aspects of a level and give them dedicated focus, rather than rushing to the next full level. This matches how learners actually progress — there are plateaus where consolidation matters more than new material.
- **Pre-A1 topics are mostly vocabulary-as-grammar.** At this level, grammatical structures are learned as unanalysed chunks. The curriculum should treat these as recognition/production of fixed phrases, not as rule-based grammar.
- **Completeness check:** If a French grammar point does not appear on this list, it should be added before designing the weekly cycles. This inventory should be treated as a living document.
