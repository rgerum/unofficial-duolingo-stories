# French Grammar Sequence — Weekly Curriculum Cycles

Generated from `french-grammar-curriculum.md` (topic inventory) and `french-grammar-sequencing-principles.md` (ordering rules).

Each sublevel has its own independent cycle. When a cycle ends, it restarts from week 1 with different news/story content.

**Legend:**
- **I** = Introduction week (new topic)
- **R** = Review week (recycles recent topics)
- Secondary topics are from prior weeks in the cycle or from a lower sublevel (reinforce/deepen mode).

### Notes

- **A1 is the longest cycle** at 27 weeks (~6 months). This is by design — A1 carries the most foundational grammar. On cycle repetition, learners encounter the same grammar sequence with fresh news content, reinforcing through spaced repetition.
- **Total path Pre-A1 → C2 is ~34 months** assuming one cycle per level with no repetition. In practice, learners may repeat cycles or skip levels, so real timelines vary.
- **Secondary topics favour recency** — the most recently introduced compatible topic is preferred, creating tighter spaced repetition loops.
- **Review weeks recycle 2–4 topics** from the preceding block. Story content during review weeks should blend these topics naturally rather than drilling them explicitly.
- **Cross-level deepening** is marked "(deepen)" in the secondary column. This signals to the story generator that the topic was covered at a lower level and can now be used in more complex contexts.
